Laplacian operator on the graph domain
======
	Laplacian operator in real domain is simply the second derivative of function f,
f^'' (x)=lim_(h→0)⁡〖(f(x+h)-2f(x)+f(x-h))/h^2 〗
	For discretized the real line by its dyadic points,  
f^'' (x)=lim_(h→0)⁡〖(f(x+1/2^n )-2f(x)+f(x-1/2^n ))/(1/2^n )^2 〗
	This is the sum of all the differences of f(x) with f evaluated at all it’s neighbors (and then proper renormalized). Thus, the pointwise formulation for the Laplacian on a function f is,
△f(x)= ∑_(y~x)▒〖f(x)-f(y)〗
	For a finite graph, the Laplacian can be represented as a matrix,
L=D-A,
where D denoted NxN degree matrix of A, and A denoted NxN adjacency matrix. The matrix L is called the unweighted Laplacian. 
	The renormalized Laplacian is defined as L_renormalized=D^(-1/2) LD^(1/2).
	The Laplace-Beltrami operator is a generalization of the Laplacian (defined on the Euclid space) to Riemannian manifolds.
